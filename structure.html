structure of a coronavirus



There are four structural proteins:

Spike proteins - These proteins give way to the name "corona" meaning crown. It primarily aids in the attachment of the virus to the host cell via a receptor called ACE2 or Angiotensin Converting Enzyme. This receptor is found in the lungs, heart, kidneys, and intestines.

Envelope proteins - This protein is the smallest and is critical in the assembly and release of new virus cells from host cells.

Membrane proteins - This protein is the most abundant and primarily acts as the central organizer.

Nucleocapsid proteins- This protein encapsulates the single stranded RNA and mainly inhibits the defense mechanisms of the host and assists in replication.

Because of the structure of the viral envelope, hand washing with soap remains one of the most effective methods of protection against the SARS-CoV-2 virus and all enveloped viruses. Do you remember biology classes talking about cell membranes? Well, cells have what's called a phospholipid bilayer made up of tiny phospholipids. These phospholipids have a hydrophobic tail and a hydrophillic head, essentially meaning that the heads like water and the tails don't. There are two layers made up of these parts! Soap has the ability to break apart this membrane, hence why it's so effective in cleaning.
