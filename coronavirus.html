what is a coronavirus?


  You've probably seen this picture at some point or another. The thing is, “coronavirus” isn't the most precise term for the virus causing the COVID-19 pandemic. This is because coronaviruses are actually a group of viruses.



   Coronaviruses are nothing new. They can cause mild to moderate upper-respiratory tract illnesses.

  Have you ever caught a cold? There’s a good chance that it was caused by a coronavirus. There are hundreds of coronaviruses, many circulating amongst animals, but in something called a spillover event, the virus makes its way to humans.
There have been a total of seven known coronavirus that have infected humans.
  There are three total recorded instances of highly pathogenic coronaviruses to infect humans; SARS in 2002 (which stands for Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) caused by the SARS-CoV virus, and MERS in 2012 (short for Middle East Respiratory Syndrome). The third is our current situation, COVID-19 caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, named aptly due to the similarities it shars with the Sars-CoV virus.
