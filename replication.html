replication

Did you know that the SARS-CoV-2 virus has around 30,000 nucleotides in its RNA genome? That's a very large genome, especially for a virus!
Some of the notable things about this virus are that its ribosome frameshifts during translation and that it produces multiple subgenomic RNAs. It does this through a process called discontinuous transcription. This means that the virus is able to use the one RNA strand to produce several different proteins with the help of TRSs (or Transcription-Regulating Sequences). There are two main TRSs: TRS-L or leader TRS and cTRS-B or body TRS.
